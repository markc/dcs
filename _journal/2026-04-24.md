## md.js frontmatter strip

- After the `_spec/` files started appearing in the in-page doc viewer, their YAML frontmatter rendered as a leading paragraph of pseudo-text (md.js has no frontmatter awareness and its `^[-*_]{3,}\s*$` rule treated the `---` lines as `<hr>`).
- Surveyed conventions: **CommonMark / GFM** don't define frontmatter; **GitHub's web UI** renders it as a two-column table (unique); **Jekyll / Hugo / Zola / Astro / MDX / Pandoc** all strip it as metadata. The SSG / doc-tool convention is overwhelmingly "strip".
- Shipped the strip-only approach вЂ” 3-line addition at the top of `md()`: `s = s.replace(/^---\r?\n[\s\S]*?\r?\n---\r?\n?/, '')`. Matches Jekyll/Hugo/Pandoc, avoids bringing in a YAML parser (which would violate the zero-deps invariant), and the frontmatter is still visible in the raw `.md` files anyway.
